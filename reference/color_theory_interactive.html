Part 04 — Color Models

RGB vs CMYK.
Light vs Ink.

Two completely different systems for making color — one uses light, the other uses ink. They work in the opposite way.

💡
RGB
Additive — starts from black
Starts with darkness and adds light. All three at full = white. Used in screens, TVs, monitors, cameras.
R+G+B = White · Additive mixing
🖥️ Screens📱 Phones📸 Cameras💡 LEDs
🖨️
CMYK
Subtractive — starts from white
Starts with white paper and subtracts light by layering inks. All three = near-black (K adds true black). Used in printing.
C+M+Y = Black · Subtractive mixing
🖨️ Printing📰 Magazines📦 Packaging

Part 06 — HSB / HSL Color Studio

Hue. Saturation.
Brightness. Value.

The HSB model describes color the way humans see it. Drag each slider to discover exactly how Hue, Saturation, Brightness, and Value transform a color.

🎨 Hue 200°
The pure color — its angle on the color wheel (0°–360°). 0°=Red, 120°=Green, 240°=Blue.
💧 Saturation 80%
How vivid or gray — 0% is pure gray, 100% is the most vivid version of the hue.
☀️ Brightness 70%
How dark or bright — 0% is always black, 100% at full saturation = pure vivid color.
🌑 Value (Shading) 0%
How much black is mixed in — 0% = pure color, 100% = full black. This is the "Value" in color theory, controlling shade depth.
🌙 Lightness (HSL) 55%
HSL lightness — 0%=black, 50%=pure hue, 100%=white. Different model from brightness.

Part 08 — Color Temperature

Warm vs Cool.
The feel of color.

Colors have a perceived temperature. Warm colors advance and energize. Cool colors recede and calm. This deeply affects emotional response to any design.

← Cool (10,000K) Blues/PurplesNeutral White (5,500K)Warm (1,800K) Reds/Oranges →
❄️ Cool Colors
Blues, purples, cool greens. Recede, feel distant, calm, professional. Used in tech & healthcare.
5,000K – 10,000K
🌤️ Neutral
Whites with a warm cast, cream tones. Feel natural and inviting like midday sunlight.
~4,000–5,500K (Daylight)
🔥 Warm Colors
Yellows, oranges, reds. Advance toward the viewer, feel energetic, urgent, and inviting.
2,700K – 4,000K
🌋 Hot Colors
Deep reds and warm crimsons. Most intense warm tones — passionate, alarming, powerful.
1,800K – 2,200K
🌿 Neutral Hue
Greens sit in the middle — neither warm nor cool. Feel balanced, natural, and organic.
Context-dependent
🎨 Mixing Temps
Cool + warm in one palette creates dynamic tension. Great for highlighting contrast in UI.
Designer's choice

Part 13 — Black & White

Expression
without color.

B&W strips color to reveal form, contrast, and value. Adjust sliders to see how brightness, contrast, and grain change the expression of a composition.

Why B&W matters
Without color, viewers focus purely on shape, form, and contrast. Designers test layouts in B&W first — if it reads well without color, it will work with any palette.
